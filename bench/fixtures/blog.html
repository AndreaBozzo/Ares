Rethinking Backpressure in Async Rust

By Mara Vinçek on May 14, 2026

Backpressure is the quiet contract that keeps async systems from melting down under load. This post walks through how bounded channels, poll_ready, and cooperative yielding combine to propagate slowness instead of dropping work — and where the common Tokio patterns leak that contract.

When a producer outruns a consumer, something has to give. Unbounded queues trade memory for the illusion of progress; bounded queues trade latency for stability. We argue the second trade is almost always the right one for long-running services.

The mechanics matter. A tokio::sync::mpsc channel with a capacity of one behaves very differently from one with a capacity of 1024, and the difference shows up not in throughput but in tail latency under saturation.
