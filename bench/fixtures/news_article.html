City Council Approves Downtown Transit Overhaul

  • transit
  • city-council
  • budget

The Meridian city council voted 7–2 on Tuesday to fund a four-year redesign of downtown bus and tram lines, the largest transit investment in two decades.

The $310 million plan adds two dedicated tram corridors, rebuilds eleven stations for step-free access, and shifts night service to a 20-minute frequency. Supporters argued it would cut commute times; the two dissenting members questioned the funding timeline.

Construction is expected to begin next spring, with the first corridor opening in late 2028. The city will hold public consultations through August.
